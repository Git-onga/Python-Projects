5th Annual Science and Tech Conference Kicks Off Tomorrow

By James Mwangi October 9, 2023 14 comments

The university is buzzing with anticipation as final preparations are made for the 5th Annual Science and Technology Conference, which begins tomorrow morning at the main auditorium. This year's theme, "Innovation for Sustainable Development," has attracted record numbers of participants from across the region.

Dr. Wanjiru Mwangi, the keynote speaker and renowned AI researcher, arrived on campus yesterday to prepare for her opening address. "I'm thrilled to be back at KyU," she told our reporter. "The work being done here in sustainable technology is truly groundbreaking."

Conference Highlights

The three-day event will feature:

  • Over 50 presentations from leading researchers
  • Hands-on workshops in emerging technologies
  • A student innovation showcase
  • Networking opportunities with industry leaders

The conference chair, Professor Kamau from the Computer Science department, emphasized the importance of this year's theme. "With climate change and resource scarcity being critical global issues, we wanted to focus on how technology can provide sustainable solutions," he explained.

"This conference represents the best of what KyU has to offer - cutting-edge research with real-world impact." - Vice Chancellor Dr. Nyambura

Student Participation

Over 100 KyU students will be presenting their research, including third-year engineering student James Kariuki, whose solar-powered irrigation system won last month's innovation competition. "This is an incredible opportunity to share my work with experts in the field," Kariuki said.

The conference is open to all university students and faculty. Registration can be completed at the auditorium entrance each morning starting at 8:00 AM.
